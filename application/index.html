Help

Sound Preset

Overview

With sound presets, you can quickly save and reapply sound adjustments.

Saving a Preset

To save the current sound adjustments as a new preset, select Save New Preset. Type a name for the preset and press the checkmark. Your preset is now saved.

Applying a Preset

To apply a preset, select it from the list and then choose Apply All Adjustments.

You can also uncheck adjustments that you don't want to apply.

If changes have been made since saving or applying the last preset, you can save the changes to that preset or create a new one.

Preset Contents

A sound preset contains the following adjustments:

  • Loudspeaker roles, mute states and levels
  • Loudspeaker dimensions (BeoCreate Elements only)
  • Crossover settings (loudspeaker type)
  • Equaliser filters
  • ToneTouch settings

BeoCreate 4-Channel Amplifier

Channels

BeoCreate 4-Channel Amplifier has four channels. In the application, they are indicated with colours and letters A–D, shown above.

Connection Tips

For the best performance, connect drivers as follows:

  • Large drivers, such as woofers, to channels A and B
  • Small drivers, such as tweeters, to channels C and D

Remember to use the correct polarity (plus and minus).

Loudspeaker Type

Overview

Selecting the correct loudspeaker types will improve the sound and protect the drivers.

Selecting a Loudspeaker Type

To choose a loudspeaker type, tap the picture of the loudspeaker driver. From the panel, select the closest match to what is connected to the amplifier. A crossover is then automatically set up.

A crossover splits the sound spectrum to bands, sending each of them to the appropriate loudspeaker driver. This protects the drivers from signals they are not designed to play.

Loudspeaker Channels

Advanced Crossover

The automatic crossover is a general approximation. To customise the crossover for your loudspeaker, use the sliders in Crossover & Filters screen.

Muting a Channel

To mute or unmute a channel, tap the loudspeaker icon at the far right. This control is also available on the Level tab.

Loudspeaker Role

Overview

Select how a stereo signal is distributed to the loudspeakers.

Selecting a Role

Tap on an option to assign a role to the loudspeaker channel:

  • Left plays the left channel of a stereo signal.
  • Right plays the right channel of the signal.
  • Mono downmixes the left and right channels of the signal together.

Loudspeaker Level

Overview

Adjust the relative levels of the loudspeaker channels.

Adjusting Levels

Drag the sliders from right to left to lower the volume of a channel.

For maximum sound quality, only lower the volume of channels that are too loud. Always use the volume control of the source to adjust overall volume of the system.

Muting a Channel

To mute or unmute a channel, tap the loudspeaker icon to the right of the slider. This control is also available on the Type tab.

More Options

Ambience

Determines whether the channel receives ToneTouch ambience effect. Depending on the loudspeaker configuration, you may want to turn it off for certain channels.

Adjust the ambience level in ToneTouch screen by scaling the dot with two fingers.

ToneTouch

Overview

ToneTouch is a quick and intuitive way to customise the tonality of the sound system.

Bass and Treble

Moving the dot adjusts bass and treble.

  • Drag the dot right to boost treble, left to reduce it.
  • Drag the dot up to boost bass, down to reduce it.

When the dot is at the center, the adjustments are off.

Ambience

If your system has a single loudspeaker, you may want to try increasing ambience of the sound.

Spread with two fingers to make the dot larger, pinch to make it smaller. The larger the dot, the more ambience is added to the sound.

When the dot is the smallest, only the original sound is heard, without added ambience.

By default, the ambience effect applies to all loudspeaker channels. It can be disabled for selected channels on the More tab of the Loudspeaker Setup screen.

Technical Information

ToneTouch does not add any artificially created effects to the sound, such as reverb or echo. All elements in the "ambience signal" are present in the original sound.

  • Most of the signal is created by substracting the left channel from the right channel.
  • Below 300 Hz, the signal is a sum of the left and right channels to preserve bass.

When ambience is increased, more of this signal is mixed together with the original sound.

Bass and treble adjustments are applied before the equalisation filters that can be adjusted in the Filters screen.

Room Compensation

Overview

Room Compensation adapts the loudspeakers to the listening space.

Making Measurements

Measurements tell the system how the listening space influences the sound.

First measurement is the reference – how the loudspeaker sounds like up close.

Graphs

Overview

The graph is a visual representation of how the system processes sound. Note that it does not visualise the music that is played. The next sections will help you read and understand the graphs.

Colours

Colours match the four available loudspeaker channels. There are two sets of information shown at the same time on the grid:

  • Bright curves show the magnitude response of the channels (also often called frequency response).
  • Dim curves indicate the phase response of the channels.
Loudspeaker Channels

When an individual channel is selected, the curves of other channels will be dimmed to make the selected channel stand out.

Magnitude Response

Indicates the frequency-dependent loudness of output signal relative to the input signal. Frequency ("pitch") of the signal is shown on the horizontal axis, from 10 to 20000 Hz.

  • "Bass" is everything below about 250 Hz on the graph.
  • "Midrange" is between about 250 and 2000 Hz.
  • "Treble" is everything above about 2000 Hz.

Intensity of the signal is shown on the vertical axis, from +15 to –15 dB. When the graph is above the centerline, the output signal is louder than the input signal. Conversely, the signal is quieter below the centerline, and unchanged at 0 dB.

Phase Response

Phase is a frequency-dependent time delay in the signal. It is affected by the filters that are active. Frequency of the signal is again shown on the horizontal axis. Phase shift is shown in degrees on the vertical axis, from -180° to +180°.

  • At 0°, the output signal is in phase with the input signal.
  • At 180°, the output signal is inverted in relation to the input signal.
  • At 360°, the output signal is again in phase with the input signal, though delayed by one full cycle (a full back-and-forth movement of the loudspeaker driver).

The graph will "roll over" to indicate phase shifts beyond 180°. In this case, the curve is cut at the edge of the grid and will continue from the opposite edge.

The phase response graph is helpful when using certain types of crossover filters. Tap the help button next to Crossover for more information.

Filters

Overview

Filters adapt the loudspeaker drivers to their enclosures by boosting or attenuating parts of the sound spectrum. They look like peaks and valleys on the graph.

Each channel has 10 equaliser filters available. Select a filter from the list to adjust its settings. The selected filter is shown as a filled section of the graph with a dot.

Presets

Filters are always customisable, but selecting a Sound Preset will apply predefined settings. Refer to help in the Sound Preset screen for more information.

Adjust a Filter

To quickly adjust the selected filter, drag anywhere on the graph.

  • Drag left or right to adjust the center frequency.
  • Drag up or down to adjust intensity.
  • A Pinch or spread gesture makes the filter narrower or wider.

To fine-tune the adjustments, tap the boxes with values below the filter list. Double-tap to "smart round" the values.

To turn a filter off or on, select it from the list and tap on it again. A diagonal line indicates the filter is off.

Copy and Paste

All filters are independent of each other. To use the same settings for other filters quickly, use copy and paste.

  • Tap and hold on an individual filter in the filter list to copy and paste individual filters.
  • Tap and hold on the channel letter below the graph to copy and paste all filters of that channel. You can also paste just one filter this way – the settings will be applied to the currently selected filter on that channel.

The last settings you copy will be preserved on the pasteboard, even if you copy other things (such as text or pictures) on your device.

About Filters

The available filters are 2nd-order single-precision infinite impulse response (IIR) filters.

Crossover

Overview

Crossover divides the sound spectrum between loudspeaker drivers, so that each driver only plays the the frequencies they are designed for.

Colours of the sliders match the four available loudspeaker channels.

Loudspeaker Channels

Presets

Crossover settings are always customisable, but the following features will apply predefined settings:

  • Selecting a Sound Preset.
  • Selecting loudspeaker types in Speaker Type & Role screen.

Refer to help of these features for more information.

Adjust the Crossover

Sliders quickly narrow down the frequency band.

  • Drag the left handle to adjust the lower limit of the frequency band (high-pass filter).
  • Drag the right handle to adjust the upper limit of the frequency band (low-pass filter).
  • Drag the handle all the way to the end to turn off the filter completely. When there is no value above the handle, the filter is off.

The handle will briefly "snap" to the values of other sliders to ease aligning the filters.

Tap the high-pass and low-pass symbols to fine-tune the filters.
